The Dart

Primary

A folded-paper dart, viewed from above. The same shape drives the brand mark and the driver pin on the map — one form, one identity. The crease catches light to imply altitude and motion. Two-tone fold reads as a paper plane or compass needle depending on context.

Route-D Monogram

Alt 01

A capital D where the curved side traces a route, opening at the top into a small pickup dot. Reads as a wayfinding mark — the rider boards at the dot and the route arcs around.

djera

DJ Ligature

Alt 02

The "dj" digraph fused into a single glyph: the descender of the J swings under the d to form a road. Type-driven, no separate icon needed — works at sign-size and at favicon-size as just dj.

The Seed

Alt 03

A nod to the name — "jeera" / "djera" is cumin in Arabic. An almond-shaped seed with a single ridge down the middle; warm, organic, distinct from every other rideshare's geometric circles. Sits beautifully next to Arabic letterforms.
